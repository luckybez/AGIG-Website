07 · Practical questions

What responsible buyers ask before they proceed.

Why not just use ChatGPT or another artificial-intelligence tool?

A general tool can draft or answer. It does not by itself create governed continuity across your approved sources, decisions, commitments, people, permissions, and later changes. Artificial intelligence is one bounded mechanism inside the system—not the system or authority.

How much work will this require from me?

You participate where authority matters: approving sources, defining principles and boundaries, correcting context, training the workflow, reviewing outputs, and accepting the result. Avant Garde carries the architecture, configuration, and supported refinement.

What information does the system access?

Only sources approved for the bounded domain. Minimum necessary access is the default. Credentials remain client-controlled unless a separate support arrangement explicitly states otherwise.

What happens when models or providers change?

The governed source record, permissions, operating rules, and review standards remain distinct from any one model. Providers and tools can be reassessed or replaced without redefining the client’s authority.

Does the system make decisions?

No. It may preserve, compare, prepare, monitor, or draft within agreed permissions. Consequential decisions and actions remain with the accountable person.

What happens after the first deployment?

The initial workflow is reviewed against agreed acceptance checks, then refined with support. Expansion happens only when the bounded system has proved reliable and both parties agree.

How is privacy shared between Avant Garde, providers, and the client?

Avant Garde defines architecture and boundaries; providers are responsible for their own infrastructure programs; the client remains responsible for account, device, credential, and access discipline. Provider claims are not Avant Garde certifications.

Security and trust principles

Minimum necessary access. Connect only the sources required for the agreed domain.

Client-controlled credentials. Sensitive credentials are entered by the client and are not retained unless a separate arrangement explicitly requires it.

Visible authority. Monitoring, drafting, preparation, approval, and action are distinct. Boundaries are documented.

Source-aware work. Important claims remain traceable to evidence, with uncertainty and conflict visible.

Provider precision. Infrastructure providers publish their own security and compliance programs. Those claims apply to the provider and are not Avant Garde certifications.

Reversible expansion. New sources and workflows are added only after review. Access can be narrowed or removed.

Shared discipline. Security depends on sound architecture, client account and device protection, careful use, regular review, and honest escalation.
