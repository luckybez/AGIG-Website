Practical questions

The questions worth asking before you proceed.

Why not just use ChatGPT or another artificial-intelligence tool?

A general tool can draft or answer. It does not keep your decisions, commitments, people, and changes connected, and it does not know what you approved it to see. The intelligence is one part inside the system. It is not the system, and it is not the authority.

Why not hire an assistant?

An assistant adds capacity; this keeps the record. An assistant leaves, forgets, and needs the same reconstruction you do. The system keeps the reasons and follow-through connected, and it is there at two in the morning.

How much work will this require from me?

You participate where authority matters: approve sources, set boundaries, correct context, train the workflow, and accept the result. Avant Garde handles the design, configuration, and refinement.

What information does the system access?

Only sources approved for the defined domain. Minimum necessary access is the default. Credentials remain under your control unless a separate support arrangement explicitly states otherwise.

What happens when models or providers change?

Your source record, access rules, working rules, and review standards remain separate from any one model. Providers and tools can be reassessed or replaced without redefining your authority.

Does the system make decisions?

No. It may preserve, compare, prepare, monitor, or draft within agreed permissions. Consequential decisions and actions remain with the accountable person.

What happens after the first deployment?

The first workflow is tested against agreed checks, then refined with support. Scope grows only after the system has proved reliable and both parties agree.

How is privacy shared between Avant Garde, providers, and the client?

Avant Garde defines the design and boundaries; providers are responsible for their own infrastructure programs; the client remains responsible for account, device, credential, and access discipline. Provider claims are not Avant Garde certifications.

Security and trust principles

Minimum necessary access. Connect only the sources required for the agreed domain.

Client-controlled credentials. Sensitive credentials are entered by the client and are not retained unless a separate arrangement explicitly requires it.

Visible authority. Monitoring, drafting, preparation, approval, and action are distinct. Boundaries are documented.

Source-aware work. Important claims remain traceable to evidence, with uncertainty and conflict visible.

Provider precision. Our infrastructure providers publish their own enterprise security and compliance programs. Those programs apply to the provider. They are not an Avant Garde certification.

Reversible expansion. New sources and workflows are added only after review. Access can be narrowed or removed.

Shared discipline. Security depends on sound design, client account and device protection, careful use, regular review, and honest escalation.
