High-stakes medical decision · Built for himself first

The Founder's Own Call

Before Avant Garde was a company, the founder faced an irreversible eye-surgery decision: three possible procedures and specialists who disagreed. Scattered across the table sat consult notes, imaging, clinical research, and three conflicting recommendations.

He built a private decision layer to carry it: options compared, sourced fact separated from assumption, unaddressed risks surfaced, and the questions each surgeon needed to answer, prepared in advance. He walked into the deciding consultation with the tradeoffs mapped and chose with clear eyes.

The system he built for that one decision became the blueprint for everything Avant Garde installs.

Decision-support only. The choice remained his, made with his qualified medical team.

High-stakes medical decision

The Informed Patient

Roger was evaluating a serious prostate-related decision with irreversible consequences. What he had was more than three months of fragmented research: charts, studies, conversations, and specialist input that never sat on one desk at the same time.

Buried in that fragmentation: one treatment path carried a roughly 70 percent risk of erectile dysfunction that had nearly gone unexamined, and a cancer-safety question that would otherwise have been missed.

His system put a preparation brief on the desk: options, risks, tradeoffs, and the questions his doctors needed to answer. He raised the risk and the safety question directly, and made an informed choice with his medical team.

Decision-support only. The system does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace qualified medical judgment.

Legacy & succession

The Philanthropist

A founder had decades of giving behind him and a family who would carry it forward. The reasoning lived almost entirely in his memory: manuscript material, philanthropic intent, values, family conversations, and forty years of decisions. His family could inherit the assets. The judgment behind them had nowhere durable to live.

A private stewardship layer connected his giving logic, values, family alignment, and decision history into one place. The work shifted from memoir to continuity of judgment: the principles behind the family's next chapter became something they could hold.

Family support

A Father Raising an Autistic Son

The moments that mattered most happened at home, with the people he loves, and left no time to search. What he knew was scattered: behavioral patterns, routines, triggers, specialist guidance, and what worked last time, spread across memory and notes.

His system kept it ready: patterns organized, routines on hand, trusted guidance and next actions prepared before the moment arrived. Preparation replaced reaction. Steadier responses, calmer moments, and a father better equipped to support his son with dignity and care.
