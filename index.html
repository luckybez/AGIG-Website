The Decision Desk · Prepared brief

Decision: Renew the Hartwell building lease, or consolidate into the east facility

Prepared Tuesday · for Thursday's call

What changed
The landlord's renewal terms arrived Friday: +18% over three years. The east facility's renovation finished under budget.
Relevant history
You considered consolidating in 2024 and deferred — the deciding reason was the Hartwell team's commute. That reason was recorded, and two of the three people it concerned have since relocated.
Commitments
You told the Hartwell staff in March there would be no changes "this year." A renewal decision after December keeps that promise; a move before it does not.
Sourced facts
Renewal terms (landlord letter, Fri). East-facility capacity report (May). Current lease exit clause: 90-day notice, no penalty after Q3.
Assumptions to examine
"The team will resist the move" — based on 2024 conversations, not current ones. "The east facility can absorb everyone" — the capacity report assumed current headcount, not next year's plan.
Risks
Consolidation risks losing the two senior staff who anchored the 2024 deferral. Renewal locks the higher cost through the period when the succession question lands.
Tradeoffs
Renewal buys stability and keeps the promise, at a known cost. Consolidation frees capital and simplifies operations, at a people cost that is currently assumed, not verified.
Perspectives
Your accountant would examine the exit-clause timing. A facilities advisor would question the capacity assumption. The person you trust on people matters would ask what the team actually thinks now.
Open questions
What do the current Hartwell staff actually prefer? Does the landlord have room on the 18%? Does next year's plan change the capacity math?

Authority The decision is yours.

Next action Three questions to settle before Thursday's call — drafted and ready.

10 · Privacy, security, and human authority

Protect what is entrusted.

Your system is built around a durable source of truth, not a collection of disconnected AI conversations. Important context, decisions, commitments, and approved records remain governed in the environment designed to hold them.

AI tools operate within that environment. They do not become the authority.

Controlled access
Access is limited to the people and systems authorized to use it. Permissions and information boundaries are defined according to the sensitivity of the work.
Strong account protection
Multi-factor authentication, strong passwords, protected devices, and disciplined access practices remain part of the operating standard.
Inspectable reasoning
Sources, assumptions, and interpretations remain distinguishable wherever the system and source material allow. Important decisions should be reviewable, not hidden behind an unexplained answer.
Human authority
The system can organize, challenge, prepare, and remember. It does not become responsible for the decision.
Your information stays yours to give
The system learns directly from you through private onboarding and approved source connections. Access is limited to what the work requires, and sensitive credentials remain under your control.

Security is a shared discipline.

No technical control can compensate for a shared password, an unlocked device, or careless access. Avant Garde designs the infrastructure and its boundaries. You retain responsibility for your account, device, and access practices.

The system carries the context. You retain the authority.
