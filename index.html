Intelligence Augmentation in practice

Useful before impressive.

Remember

Keeps relevant history, decisions, standards, commitments, and open loops available when the work returns.

Prepare

Assembles people, records, risks, prior reasoning, and unanswered questions before the meeting or decision.

Research

Finds and compares source material, separates evidence from assumption, and identifies what still requires verification.

Challenge

Pressure-tests assumptions, surfaces risks and alternatives, and brings forward perspectives the accountable person may need to consider.

Draft

Prepares correspondence, briefs, plans, and working documents in the person’s intent and voice. Nothing leaves without approval.

Coordinate

Keeps people, dependencies, deadlines, handoffs, and commitments connected.

Monitor

Watches approved sources for meaningful change and brings forward what deserves attention rather than adding noise.

Teach

It explains its reasoning as it works, so you get sharper at directing, challenging, and deciding while the system carries the preparation.

Execute

Completes approved repeatable work within defined permissions, verifies the result where possible, and escalates exceptions or consequential choices.

Automation absorbs repetition. Intelligence Augmentation protects judgment.

It supports the work. It does not decide for you.

Practical questions

The questions worth asking before you proceed.

Why not just use a general AI chat tool?

A general tool can research, draft, and answer. Advanced prompting can make those interactions substantially better. Private cognitive infrastructure adds the continuity layer: approved sources, decisions, commitments, relationships, boundaries, changes, and operating history carried across interactions. The model is one mechanism inside the system. It is not the system, and it is not the authority. Chat history is not decision memory; active decision memory keeps decisions, commitments, and changes connected over time.

Why not hire an assistant?

An excellent assistant adds judgment, capacity, coordination, and human relationship. Cognitive infrastructure serves a different function: it preserves the operating record across tools and time. The two can work together. The system helps the principal and the people around them recover context, understand what changed, and maintain continuity without relying on one person’s memory.

How much work will this require from me?

You participate where authority matters: defining responsibilities, approving sources, setting boundaries, correcting context, training the working relationship, and accepting consequential outputs. Avant Garde handles architecture, connection, consolidation, configuration, and refinement.

What information does the system access?

Only sources you approve and that are appropriate to the purpose. The system may connect broadly across your operating world, but permissions remain explicit and minimum-necessary at the account, folder, database, workflow, and action level. Some domains may remain separate where privacy, professional authority, ethics, security, or preference requires it.

What happens when models or providers change?

Your source record, governance, permissions, working rules, decisions, and review standards remain separate from any one model. Models and interfaces can be reassessed or replaced without surrendering the underlying continuity layer.

Does the system make decisions?

No. It can preserve, compare, prepare, monitor, draft, challenge, coordinate, and execute approved repeatable work within defined permissions. Consequential decisions and accountability remain with the person who owns them.

What happens after the system is connected?

The connections are the foundation. Trust is earned through accurate work. The client becomes more fluent in directing and reviewing the system, recognizes more use cases, develops stronger working practices, and learns how to use challenge and teachable moments to improve judgment. The system becomes more useful through approved corrections, decisions, preferences, and operating history.

How is privacy shared between Avant Garde, providers, and the client?

Avant Garde defines the architecture, access boundaries, and working rules. Providers remain responsible for their own infrastructure and compliance programs. The client remains responsible for account, device, credential, permission, and use discipline. Provider certifications do not become Avant Garde certifications.

Trust principles

Permission before connection. Connect approved sources that are relevant to the client’s operating world, using purpose-bound and minimum-necessary permissions at each source and action layer.

Human authority. Consequential choices, external communication, and exceptions remain subject to the client’s approval.

Traceable work. Material claims point to sources; uncertainty and missing evidence stay visible.

Reversible access. Connections, permissions, and workflows can be reviewed, narrowed, paused, or removed without surrendering the underlying source record.

Shared responsibility. Avant Garde, providers, and the client each retain responsibility for their part of the environment. Provider certifications do not become Avant Garde certifications.
