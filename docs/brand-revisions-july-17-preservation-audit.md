## Executive finding

The July 17 rebuild is a stronger homepage. It has a clearer opening, a more coherent causal path, a bounded first deployment, a single dominant CTA, better responsive behavior, and a disciplined visual system.

The cost of that simplification is material. The current page explains the problem and the operating model, but it provides less evidence that Avant Garde can serve a whole operating world over time. It removed:

- the practical range expressed by Remember, Prepare, Research, Teach, and Assist;
- the memoir working brief, which demonstrated support beyond executive decision preparation;
- the only named client proof and testimonial;
- daily, family, philanthropic, and personal-history applications;
- the white-glove implementation, training, and support promise;
- a compact founder credibility module on the homepage.

Restoring all of that to the homepage would recreate the original density problem. The recommended course is selective preservation:

1. keep the new narrative spine;
2. restore practical breadth in one compact module;
3. restore implementation depth after the first-deployment section;
4. introduce one consent-cleared proof module;
5. relocate richer use cases to a dedicated page;
6. keep founder detail and lineage on About.

### Drift risks

- **Generic systems integration:** High in the hero-to-system transition if “link sources” is read as integration plumbing. Counter with principles, advisory perspectives, and human meaning.
- **Knowledge management:** Medium-high because “maintained record,” citations, revision history, and source links dominate.
- **Workflow automation:** Medium because follow-through, owners, due dates, and approvals resemble workflow software.
- **Data consolidation:** Medium because the imagery and copy emphasize bringing sources together.
- **Personal productivity:** Low-medium; Daily Brief language is absent, but fit copy can still read as executive productivity.
- **Abstract artificial-intelligence language:** Low after the terminology audit. Artificial intelligence is explicitly subordinate tooling.
- **Overly technical:** Medium in “Private IA Operating System,” governed memory, revision history, and permissions without a human example nearby.
- **Excessively decision-specific:** High. Memoir, learning, family support, legacy, and broad assistance disappeared.
- **Insufficiently consequential:** Medium. “Consequential review” appears, but named stakes and consent-cleared outcomes are absent from the homepage.

## Buyer, Champion, and Advocate audit

### Buyer

**What works**

- Fragmented operating world is recognized immediately.
- Invisible reconstruction labor is concrete.
- Current tools are shown to be insufficient without insulting the buyer’s existing stack.
- The first deployment has clear scope and safeguards.
- Human authority and access boundaries are credible.

**What remains unclear**

- What the buyer receives in the first week or first month beyond a maintained record.
- How implementation, configuration, training, and continuing support work.
- Why the investment is justified in time, risk, or decision-quality terms.
- Which risks are reduced beyond missed context: missed commitments, stale advice, avoidable rework, preparation failure, and succession loss need stronger causal treatment.
- Whether the system supports the buyer’s broader world or only formal decision reviews.

### Champion

The current site is explainable, but the language is still too doctrinal for an internal referral.

**Buyer-legible champion paragraph**

> Avant Garde builds a private Intelligence Augmentation system around the work one accountable person already does. It connects approved sources to the decisions, commitments, people, and changes they affect; prepares the current context before reviews; and keeps follow-through visible afterward. The system uses governed tools to research, draft, monitor, and challenge, but the person keeps authority. It begins with one bounded workflow and expands only after it proves accurate, useful, and trustworthy.

This paragraph should guide the next copy pass even if it is not published verbatim.

### Advocate

The current page provides standards, not evidence. An advocate can repeat that the system cites sources and preserves approvals, but cannot point to a client outcome. The original Larry material is the strongest candidate if explicit public consent, quotation approval, and claim context are documented. If not, the memoir working brief is the safest demonstrative proof because it is explicitly representative, but it should be clearly labeled as an example rather than an outcome.

Credibility gaps:

- no consent-cleared named client proof;
- no quantified baseline or observed operational change;
- no implementation artifact shown beyond generated editorial imagery;
- no concrete “what you leave with” promise in the closing conversion;
- no visible description of founder involvement after the first conversation.

### Recommended hierarchy

1. **Category:** Intelligence Augmentation
2. **Buyer-facing first description:** “a private Intelligence Augmentation system built around your world”
3. **What Avant Garde builds:** private cognitive infrastructure
4. **Outcome:** Decision Infrastructure
5. **Product expression:** unresolved brand decision between “Private IA Operating System” and the attached doctrine’s “Private AI Operating Systems”
6. **Mechanism:** artificial-intelligence tools, governed memory, source context, workflows, and reasoning support

Do not lead with either acronym. Expand Intelligence Augmentation first. Do not call the system artificial intelligence. If Travis retains “Private AI Operating Systems” as a proprietary product expression, the site must explicitly state that the product belongs to the Intelligence Augmentation category and uses artificial-intelligence tools as mechanisms; the naming contradiction remains a P0 approval decision.

## UI/UX Pro Max visual audit

The skill’s database recommended a trust-and-authority conversion pattern, spacious density, a single CTA, high contrast, editorial typography, restrained motion, responsive image handling, visible focus, sequential headings, and optimized assets. The current rebuild follows those principles. Its suggested pink conversion accent and generic security-badge pattern are not brand-appropriate and should remain rejected.

### Hierarchy and density

- The new homepage has a clear, scannable hierarchy and substantially less cognitive load than `main`.
- Nine large narrative movements are visually controlled, but the repeated headline-plus-grid pattern becomes predictable after the first deployment.
- Restored material should not return as multiple equal-weight card grids. Use one compact capability band, one proof module, and one engagement continuation.

### Section rhythm and emotional progression

- Strong opening recognition and clear intellectual progression through distinction and system.
- Emotional energy drops after first deployment because outcome, performance, trust, and fit are all abstract or standards-based.
- A human proof moment should appear between Decision Infrastructure and trust.
- The closing invitation is restrained but would convert better with the original concrete “what you leave with” promise.

### Mobile behavior and CTA prominence

- Prior QA at 375px found no horizontal overflow; the primary CTA measured 50px high.
- Mobile-specific hero and continuity assets avoid desktop crop failure.
- Header CTA remains prominent, though the shortened “Private conversation” label should be standardized with the primary CTA.
- Any restored module must remain single-column and avoid returning the original long page density.

### Accessibility

- Sequential heading structure, skip link, visible focus, native dialog, reduced-motion support, and 44px+ controls are present.
- The decorative hero image appropriately uses adjacent copy for meaning.
- The governed-continuity image has descriptive alternative text.
- Any restored testimonial must use semantic `blockquote` and attribution; any case-study metrics require contextual qualification.
