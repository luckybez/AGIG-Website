# Brand revisions — July 17: narrative and information architecture

## Narrative decision

The site will follow one continuity-led argument: the buyer already has tools, but those tools hold fragments. The buyer remains the connective tissue across systems, people, information, and time. Reconstructing that context is invisible integration labor. Avant Garde builds private cognitive infrastructure that preserves the meaning, relationships, commitments, changes, and next points of attention that decisions depend on. Repeated, governed performance turns that infrastructure into Decision Infrastructure. The accountable person remains the authority.

The customer is the hero. Artificial-intelligence tools, governed memory, source context, workflows, and reasoning support are mechanisms inside the IA system, not the product identity.

## Proposed page map

- `/` — the complete buyer journey: continuity problem, distinction, system, bounded first deployment, outcomes, trust, fit, and one dominant conversion.
- `/about.html` — founder credibility and the intellectual lineage of Intelligence Augmentation, removed from the homepage's primary conversion path.

No additional page is required for this review build. The smaller map prevents thin pages and keeps supporting doctrine available without interrupting the homepage argument.

## Homepage section order

1. Hero: name the continuity burden and the outcome.
2. Fragmentation: tools hold fragments; the buyer supplies the missing connections.
3. Distinction: stored chats versus governed decision memory; access versus operating capacity.
4. System: what private cognitive infrastructure preserves and brings forward.
5. First deployment: one bounded decision domain, explicit sources, permissions, review points, and success criteria.
6. Decision Infrastructure: preparation, sequencing, follow-through, judgment, and decision quality.
7. Proof: only consent-safe, bounded evidence; otherwise anonymized operating patterns.
8. Trust and authority: privacy, source boundaries, human approval, and earned trust.
9. Fit: who benefits and who does not.
10. Invitation: repeat the single primary conversion.

## Hero recommendation

**Recommended headline:** “Your tools hold the fragments. You hold everything together.”

**Supporting line:** Avant Garde builds private cognitive infrastructure that preserves the context, commitments, relationships, and changes your decisions depend on—so the system carries continuity while authority stays with you.

This opens with the buyer's lived burden rather than the product category. “Decision Infrastructure” is introduced as the earned strategic outcome after the mechanism is understood.

**Alternatives considered**

- “Decision Infrastructure for the work only you can own.” Clear category/outcome, but too abstract before the continuity problem is established.
- “You can delegate the work. You cannot delegate the decision.” Strong and retained, but better used as a hinge into human authority than as the opening claim.
- “It doesn't make the decision. It carries what the decision depends on.” Strong mechanism boundary and retained in the trust/authority movement.

## Content disposition

### Retain

- The two required authority lines.
- Private, founder-led positioning.
- Intelligence Augmentation lineage and founder story on the About page.
- Privacy, permission, supervised configuration, and shared-security principles.
- The practical verbs: remember, prepare, research, teach, assist.

### Rewrite

- Reframe the opening around fragmentation and invisible integration labor.
- Replace comparisons to generic artificial-intelligence tools with the sharper distinctions between stored chats and governed decision memory, and between tool access and operating capacity.
- Express capabilities as a continuity loop tied to decisions, not a feature inventory.
- Replace the long engagement sequence with a bounded first-deployment pathway.
- Describe benefits as improved preparation, sequencing, follow-through, judgment, and decision quality.

### Relocate

- Move historical lineage entirely to About.
- Keep founder biography on About and reduce homepage founder material to a short credibility note.
- Move detailed security principles into the existing accessible dialog while keeping the core trust doctrine visible.

### Remove

- Repetitive vignettes, repeated CTAs, and overlapping explanations.
- Any proof whose public consent is not explicit. Use anonymized operating patterns and clearly qualified evidence only.
- Decorative artificial-intelligence language or imagery that implies autonomous authority.

## CTA hierarchy

- Primary and dominant: **Schedule a private conversation** (`calendly_clicked`), repeated in the header, hero, and closing invitation.
- Secondary: contextual in-page links such as “See how continuity works” and the About-page link.
- No competing download, newsletter, demo, or product-tour conversion.

## Proof and objection architecture

Proof is organized around observable system performance rather than inflated outcomes:

- The system can prepare a decision brief from approved sources.
- It can preserve the relationship among decision, rationale, commitments, changes, and open questions.
- It can bring forward a due commitment or material change at the agreed review point.
- It can show sources, uncertainty, and the boundary of its authority.

Client-specific material appears only where public consent is explicit; otherwise it is anonymized or omitted. Objections are answered in sequence:

1. “I already have artificial-intelligence tools.” Tool access is not operating capacity; disconnected tools still leave integration with the buyer.
2. “Is this just saved chat history?” Stored conversations are retrieval artifacts; governed decision memory is structured, source-aware, permissioned, revisable, and connected to action.
3. “Will it make decisions for me?” No. It prepares and carries context; the accountable human approves and decides.
4. “Does it require opening everything?” No. The first deployment is bounded by domain, sources, permissions, review points, and success criteria.
5. “Why should I trust it?” Trust is earned through traceable sources, visible uncertainty, explicit approvals, and repeated performance.

## Visual thesis and Higgsfield asset plan

The visual language is editorial realism: quiet, tactile evidence of human judgment and continuity. Technology stays largely invisible. Images should show the same decision environment across time—fragments before, governed continuity after—without depicting magic or automation.

Planned bounded production:

- One desktop and one mobile crop for the hero: an accountable person at a real worktable with separated source materials and a clear human point of view.
- One desktop and one mobile continuity image: the same material reorganized into an intelligible, traceable sequence, without visible dashboards or futuristic interfaces.
- Reuse an existing consent-safe founder portrait if its provenance is already accepted; do not synthesize a founder likeness.
- Exclude robots, holograms, glowing brains, floating dashboards, neon circuitry, generic server rooms, and stock-tech poses.

Prompts, model, crops, and exclusions will be documented in the review package.

## Mobile-first behavior

- Keep the narrative order identical on mobile; never hide essential argument or trust content.
- Use a single-column flow at 375px with 16px minimum body copy, 44px minimum touch targets, and no horizontal scrolling.
- Give the hero copy priority over imagery and reserve image aspect ratios to prevent layout shift.
- Turn comparisons into stacked, explicitly labeled blocks so meaning never depends on position or color.
- Keep the sticky header compact without obscuring page anchors.
- Preserve visible focus, semantic headings, descriptive alt text, native disclosure/dialog behavior, and reduced-motion support.
- Use only restrained opacity/color transitions in the 150–300ms range; no decorative scroll choreography.
