01 · Larry

When your life does not fit inside one project, your system cannot either.

“Now I know where things stand and what needs my attention.”

The person and the world he carries

Larry Conaway is a philanthropist, fund operator, traveler, writer, correspondent, property owner, and family leader. The Conaway Impact Fund, a memoir and a second book, travel plans, financial and property obligations, correspondence, family responsibilities, and succession questions all compete for attention without becoming separate lives.

What remained his responsibility

The choices about stewardship, family, money, writing, and legacy remained Larry’s. The work was not to automate his judgment. It was to make the history, promises, changes, and unanswered questions available when that judgment was required.

What was difficult to hold

Important context was scattered across messages, documents, memories, travel plans, financial records, manuscript drafts, and other people. Urgent matters could become the organizing system, while consequential commitments waited outside the current field of view.

Why ordinary tools were insufficient

Each tool could store its piece. Isolated prompting could help with a document or question. Neither carried the relationships between the fund, books, travel, property, correspondence, finances, family, and succession—or brought the right context forward as the work changed.

What the system was built around

The system was organized around Larry’s responsibilities and rhythms: what needed attention, who was waiting, what had changed, which commitments remained open, and what context a decision or conversation depended on.

How it behaved differently

It preserved correctable context across projects, assembled the relevant operating picture before a review, and carried commitments and changes forward. Larry could correct the record, approve sources, and decide what mattered next.

What became more visible

Larry described being able to put his finger on his taxes, projects, books, travel, and money owed to him. He called himself “ten times more efficient” than one hundred days earlier. That language is Larry’s personal assessment of the change he experienced.

What remained human—and what it proves

Larry remained the author, steward, family leader, and decision-maker. The story shows that Intelligence Augmentation becomes valuable when it can carry a whole operating world without reducing the person to a workflow.

Claims boundary

Larry’s statements are attributed to him and are not Avant Garde performance guarantees. Outcomes vary by person, implementation quality, source quality, participation, and disciplined use.

02 · Roger

The decisions changed. The person and what mattered carried forward.

The person and the changing world

Roger first needed to prepare for a consequential medical conversation. Later, his attention moved to relocation, business analysis, financial considerations, and a future restaurant project. The subjects changed, but his priorities, history, constraints, and way of evaluating options remained connected.

What remained his responsibility

Roger remained responsible for the questions he asked, the professionals he consulted, the alternatives he considered, and the decisions he made about his health, location, money, and future work.

What was difficult to reconcile

Different advisers and source materials offered different frames. The hard part was not obtaining another answer. It was preserving what had already been learned, distinguishing evidence from assumption, and carrying prior reasoning into a new but related decision.

Why isolated prompting was insufficient

A strong prompt could deepen one analysis. It could not, by itself, preserve Roger’s evolving record, maintain boundaries between professional advice and personal judgment, or carry his decision principles into later work.

What the system was built around

The system organized source material, alternatives, open questions, priorities, assumptions, and prior decisions. It prepared better questions and comparisons while keeping professional and human authority visible.

How it behaved differently

When the subject moved from medical preparation to relocation and business planning, the infrastructure did not treat Roger as a new user. It carried forward the relevant history and working pattern while keeping unrelated or sensitive context appropriately separated.

What became more manageable

Options, assumptions, and unanswered questions became easier to inspect. Roger could approach a new responsibility with continuity instead of rebuilding his way of thinking from the beginning.

What remained human—and what it proves

Professionals retained their professional roles. Roger retained the decisions. The story shows that private cognitive infrastructure can support continuity across changing responsibilities without pretending those responsibilities are interchangeable.

Claims boundary

This story concerns preparation, comparison, and continuity. It makes no claim about diagnosis, treatment efficacy, financial performance, or guaranteed outcomes.

03 · Horacio

A father asked for help becoming a better parent to his autistic son.

The person and the responsibility

Horacio is the father of an autistic son. He came to Avant Garde with one objective, stated in his own words: to be a serious, better parent. That responsibility lived across family history, daily routines, values, cultural context, specialist perspectives, and the moments at home that demand the most of him.

What was difficult to hold

An autistic child’s responses do not always explain themselves. A behavior can be a signal of overload, a disrupted routine, or something that worked yesterday and not today. Horacio had to decode those signals in real time, often at the end of a long and stressful day, while managing his own anger and frustration so that his reaction would not become part of the problem.

What he actually needed

Not another parenting article, and not advice that ignored his family. He needed support that is available in the moment it is needed: help reading what his son’s behavior may be communicating, help preparing for the situations that repeat, and a private place to work through his own state first, so the father his son meets is the calm one.

Why ordinary tools were insufficient

A generic answer could not know this family, distinguish observation from interpretation, retain cultural realities, or remember what was tried last month and what preceded the last hard evening. A collection of notes could preserve fragments without preparing him for the next moment.

What the system was built around

Avant Garde built a private decision-support council around the family: parenting, behavioral, psychological, routine, and cultural perspectives in sequence; evidence standards; his values; and continuity across incidents, so patterns, attempted approaches, and open questions stay connected. It is available at ten at night after a hard day, not only at appointment hours.

How it behaved differently

It helped him decode rather than react: separate what he observed from what he assumed, see a behavior against prior patterns, prepare the next conversation or routine adjustment, and process his own frustration before walking back into the room.

What remained human—and what it proves

The system did not parent, diagnose, or treat. Decisions, care, and relationship remained Horacio’s, in conversation with qualified professionals where appropriate. Supporting his son could not be separated from supporting the father’s own steadiness—the family weakens if the accountable parent burns out. The story shows Intelligence Augmentation carrying the most human responsibility there is, precisely by refusing to assume it.

Claims boundary

This story makes no therapeutic, clinical, or behavioral-outcome claim. The system does not diagnose, treat, or replace qualified professionals. Personal detail is limited to what is needed to explain the responsibility and the system’s role.

04 · Devin

The old system analyzed a deal. The new system carried the strategy behind it.

The person and the strategy

Devin was evaluating real-estate opportunities. The immediate task was property analysis, but the responsibility extended beyond a single deal to goals, finances, constraints, prior decisions, assumptions, portfolio thinking, and lessons that should inform the next opportunity.

What remained his responsibility

Devin remained responsible for diligence, professional advice, financial judgment, risk tolerance, and every decision to proceed or decline.

What was difficult to continue

An excellent analysis could still end when the conversation ended. The next property could require Devin to restore the same strategic context and remember which assumptions, principles, and questions had mattered before.

Why advanced prompting was insufficient

The earlier system could analyze a property through expert perspectives. It produced useful work, but the strategy remained with Devin. The interaction improved; continuity between interactions did not.

What the new system was built around

The private infrastructure was designed to carry Devin’s goals, finances, constraints, prior deals, decisions, assumptions, and lessons into future opportunities while keeping sources and decision authority explicit.

How it behaved differently

Instead of treating each property as an isolated prompt, it retained the strategic record and prepared the next analysis in that context. It could challenge a new opportunity against prior reasoning rather than merely produce another answer.

What became visible

The distinction between a powerful analysis tool and an ongoing cognitive infrastructure became clear during the demonstration. Devin chose to proceed before the full demonstration concluded.

What remained human—and what it proves

The system did not select an investment or promise a return. Devin’s decision to proceed was a decision about the infrastructure. The story shows the category movement from advanced prompting to continuity around a strategy.

Claims boundary

This is a category-recognition and implementation decision story. It does not imply an investment result, completed implementation outcome, efficiency gain, or financial performance.

The infrastructure principle

Your responsibilities may look different. The infrastructure principle is the same.

Connect what matters, preserve what future judgment depends on, and keep authority with the person who owns the consequence.
