Applications

Continuity takes different forms. Authority does not.

Private Intelligence Augmentation can support operating work, consequential research, authorship, legacy, and advisory synthesis. The system changes shape around the domain. The accountable person remains responsible.

01 · The common mechanism

Governed continuity across sources, people, commitments, decisions, responsibilities, and time.

These are not separate product packages. They are different continuity problems. Each begins with a bounded domain, approved sources, explicit permissions, reviewable outputs, and a person who retains authority.

02 · Executive operating continuity

Keep the operating picture current across projects, people, and promises.

For founders and principals whose work crosses correspondence, meetings, commitments, advisers, properties, investments, and active projects.

What is preserved
Decisions, rationale, owners, dates, dependencies, changes, and people waiting.
Recurring artifact
A Daily Brief and prepared review showing changed facts, open loops, upcoming commitments, and next actions.
Human authority
The principal sets priorities, approves actions, and decides what deserves attention.

03 · Research and consequential preparation

Prepare the questions a responsible decision requires.

For decisions where evidence is fragmented, specialists disagree, assumptions matter, and the decision-maker must arrive prepared.

What is preserved
Source material, competing views, assumptions, uncertainties, option criteria, and unresolved questions.
Recurring artifact
A cited preparation brief showing what is known, disputed, missing, or changed since the last review.
Human authority
The system does not diagnose, prescribe, provide professional advice, or make the choice.

04 · Authorship and personal history

Protect the writer’s voice from the surrounding project load.

For a memoir or personal-history project where memories, handwritten pages, recordings, drafts, research, family responsibility, and publication questions accumulate over time.

Representative working brief

Complete a memoir without turning the author into the archivist, editor, project manager, and publishing strategist.

What the person brings
Memory, voice, judgment, emotional truth, handwritten pages, recordings, and responsibility to the people represented.
What the system preserves
The thesis, intended reader, chapter map, draft history, voice rules, open questions, source material, and legacy purpose.
What the system prepares
Chapter prompts, research questions, editorial review points, accountability rhythm, rights flags, and the next useful writing step.
What remains human
The life, its meaning, the language, every sensitive inclusion, and final authorship.

05 · Philanthropy, succession, and legacy

Preserve why—not only what—across generations and handoffs.

For principals whose decisions carry history, intent, relationships, and responsibilities that should survive a change in role or generation.

What is preserved
Decision rationale, values, conditions, relationship context, prior commitments, and unresolved responsibilities.
Recurring artifact
A reviewable continuity record for stewardship, succession, or philanthropic decision-making.
Privacy boundary
Public examples remain representative and non-identifying unless explicit permission exists.

06 · Governed specialist and advisory synthesis

Keep expert perspectives distinct, comparable, and connected to the decision.

For work shaped by legal, financial, medical, technical, operational, or other qualified perspectives that should inform judgment without being flattened into one answer.

What is preserved
Who advised what, the source and date, assumptions, scope limits, conflicts, and later changes.
Recurring artifact
A comparison brief that preserves disagreement and prepares questions for qualified advisers.
Human authority
Professional advice stays with qualified professionals; the client remains responsible for the decision.

07 · Where to begin

Choose the domain with the clearest recurring burden.

The best first deployment is not the broadest. It is the domain where reconstruction is frequent, consequences are visible, sources can be bounded, and usefulness can be reviewed.
