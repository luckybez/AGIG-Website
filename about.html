About the founder

Travis Lewis

A career spent building systems for people operating under consequence: first technical, now cognitive. This is how one led to the other.

The foundation

A math kid, educated at Choate Rosemary Hall and the University of Pennsylvania, drawn early to problems where the answer has to be right, not merely persuasive.

Mission-critical

A technology career supporting mission-critical trading desks: environments where failure is expensive, pressure is real, and technology exists to serve the outcome. The real job underneath was translation. Take what the business needs, turn it into architecture, make the outcome better.

The builder

Founded Fulcrum Solutions, building advanced crypto miners end to end: the software, the hardware, and the enclosures that powered, cooled, and connected them. Machines that created value around the clock.

The trader

The machines mined altcoins, but he wanted to hold Bitcoin. Converting one into the other revealed something unexpected. When his timing, trading plan, and execution were good, the trades were almost more profitable than mining the coins in the first place. So he became a professional trader, and discovered that the weakest part of his trading plan was him.

The algorithm

So he wrote his trading philosophy into code and took himself out of the loop. The algorithm made money. The lesson stuck: judgment, engineered into a system, outperforms judgment left to feelings.

The crossroads

When AI matured, he ran the code that fueled his algorithm through AI. It optimized the code and made it more profitable. He sat with what that meant, and came to a decision: understand this capability completely, how it works, where it fails, and how to direct it, rather than be surprised by it. He had AI teach him to be a prompt engineer, became one, and saw that most of the world was still using AI like a search engine.

The proof on himself

Then the stakes turned personal: an irreversible eye-surgery decision, three possible procedures, and specialists who disagreed. He built a private decision system to carry it, walked into the deciding consultation with the tradeoffs mapped, and chose with clear eyes. Everything Avant Garde installs descends from that system. The system

He went further: he built specialist perspectives around every serious decision in his work and life, a private system for his own judgment. It pressure-tested his assumptions, surfaced his blind spots, remembered everything, and made him sharper at deciding.

The discovery

Then came the humbling part. The problem he was solving, a system to augment his judgment and gather what was scattered, was not a new idea. It carried more than a century of history: Otlet, Wells, Bush, Licklider, Engelbart. He wasn’t inventing something. He was standing on the shoulders of giants, picking up a project they could see but never build, in the first era with the infrastructure to build it. Meet them below →

Avant Garde

Avant Garde exists to share that value, deliberately, and not with everyone. It is built for decision-makers carrying decision fatigue and information overload: founders, principals, and visionaries whose calls actually change things.

The common thread

Eight generations asked the same question and hit the same wall. Judgment fails for lack of support, not lack of intelligence, and in every era, the technology could not yet build the system they could see. The idea was never wrong. The infrastructure was never ready. Now it is.

Otto’s notebook · 1998

Philosophers Andy Clark and David Chalmers imagined a man named Otto, whose memory was failing. Otto carried a notebook everywhere and wrote down everything that mattered. When he wanted to reach a museum across town, he didn’t strain to remember the address. He opened the book and walked. Their conclusion changed the philosophy of mind: the notebook wasn’t a tool Otto used. It was part of his memory, because it was always with him, instantly at hand, and trusted without a second thought.

That is the standard an Avant Garde system is built to earn.
